# Guava Healthy Leaf

## Identity
- **Status:** No disease detected
- **Host:** *Psidium guajava* L. (Guava)
- **Leaf Condition:** Normal, physiologically healthy
- **Model Output:** Healthy classification — no pathogen or pest activity detected

## Overview
A healthy guava leaf displays uniform deep-green coloration, firm texture, smooth margins, and no visible lesions, spots, discoloration, or structural abnormalities. The ViT model has classified this leaf image as healthy, indicating no current evidence of fungal, bacterial, viral, or pest-induced damage. While no treatment is required, sustained plant health demands consistent monitoring, balanced nutrition, and preventive management. The guava plant (*Psidium guajava*) is a vigorous tropical fruit tree with moderate to high susceptibility to several fungal and pest diseases; therefore, maintaining a healthy plant requires active agronomic management even in the absence of current disease.

## What a Healthy Guava Leaf Looks Like

### Visual Characteristics
- **Color:** Uniform bright to deep green (young leaves may be slightly lighter/reddish-pink initially, darkening with maturity)
- **Surface:** Smooth adaxial (upper) surface; slightly hairy (pubescent) abaxial (lower) surface
- **Venation:** Prominent midrib with 10–15 pairs of lateral veins; pinnate venation
- **Margins:** Entire (smooth), slightly wavy — no browning, scorching, or tearing
- **Texture:** Leathery, firm, slightly glossy — not wilted, crispy, or waterlogged
- **Size:** Mature leaf 7–15 cm long × 4–7 cm wide; young leaves smaller
- **Aroma:** Characteristic guava aroma when crushed (aromatic oils present)

### What Should NOT Be Present
- No dark or brown spots (fungal lesions)
- No yellow halos around spots
- No orange/yellow powdery pustules (rust)
- No holes or torn margins (insect feeding)
- No silvery/bronze streaks (thrips damage)
- No white cottony masses (mealybug)
- No black sooty deposits (sooty mold — secondary to pest infestation)
- No wilting or drooping

## Routine Care and Maintenance Protocol

### Irrigation Management
- Water requirement: 50–80 litres per tree per day during summer; reduce by 50% during monsoon
- Irrigation method: Drip irrigation preferred (reduces foliar wetness, minimizes disease risk)
- Frequency: Every 3–5 days in summer; every 7–10 days in mild weather; reduce significantly during monsoon
- Avoid waterlogging — guava is sensitive to root anaerobia; ensure well-drained soils
- Apply mulch (dried leaves, paddy straw, sugarcane trash) 5–10 cm thick around the tree base to conserve soil moisture and reduce soil splash

### Fertilizer Management
- **Nitrogen (N):** Apply 260–500 g urea per tree per year in 3 split doses (February, June, September)
- **Phosphorus (P):** Apply 250–350 g single superphosphate (SSP) per tree per year — single annual application in February
- **Potassium (K):** Apply 300–400 g muriate of potash (MOP) per tree per year in 2 splits
- **Organic matter:** Apply 30–50 kg well-decomposed farmyard manure (FDM) per tree per year before the monsoon
- **Micronutrients:** Annual foliar spray of zinc sulfate (0.3%) + boron (0.1%) during active growth phase
- Perform soil testing every 2 years; adjust fertilizer doses based on soil pH and nutrient status

### Canopy Management
- Prune after each harvest to maintain an open canopy structure
- Remove crossing branches, water shoots, and dead wood
- Target 50–60% light penetration through the canopy
- Tip-pruning of young shoots encourages lateral branching and increases fruiting surface

## Preventive Disease Management (Healthy Plant)

### Monthly Preventive Spray Calendar
| Month | Spray | Purpose |
|-------|-------|---------|
| February | Bordeaux mixture 0.5% | Pre-blossom fungal protection |
| April–May | Copper oxychloride 0.3% | Pre-monsoon protection |
| June | Mancozeb 0.25% | Monsoon disease prevention |
| July–August | Copper oxychloride 0.3% | Continue monsoon protection |
| September | Neem oil 2 ml/L + Mancozeb 0.25% | Pest + disease combined |
| October | Propiconazole 1 ml/L | Rust prevention (post-monsoon flush) |
| December–January | Micronutrient spray (Zn + B + Fe) | Nutritional maintenance |

### Pest Monitoring (Healthy Plant)
- Inspect undersides of leaves weekly for:
  - Mealybug colonies (white cottony masses)
  - Thrips (tiny, elongated insects — cause silvering)
  - Aphid colonies (green/black clusters on young shoots)
  - Fruit fly egg deposits on developing fruits
- Set yellow sticky traps (2 per acre) to monitor adult pest populations
- If pest populations exceed threshold levels — initiate IPM response

### Soil Health Monitoring
- Test soil pH annually — optimal range 6.0–7.5 for guava
- Check for root-knot nematode (*Meloidogyne* spp.) infestation — apply Carbofuran granules (3G) at 30 g per tree if detected
- Apply *Pseudomonas fluorescens* (5 g/L) as soil drench twice yearly to promote root health and suppress soilborne pathogens

## Early Warning Signs to Watch For
Even a currently healthy leaf can be on a tree beginning to develop stress. Monitor for these early warning indicators:
- **Yellow margins on older leaves** → Potassium or magnesium deficiency
- **Pale new growth** → Iron or nitrogen deficiency
- **Wilting without drought** → Root rot (Phytophthora) or nematode infestation
- **Sudden leaf drop** → Sudden temperature drop, waterlogging, or chemical spray drift
- **Abnormal leaf curling** → Aphid infestation or phytoplasma (witches' broom)
- **Gummy exudate on stems** → Early canker or bark borer damage

## Agroclimatic Requirements for Guava
- **Temperature:** 20–35°C (tolerates 10–45°C; growth inhibited below 10°C)
- **Rainfall:** 1000–2000 mm per year; can tolerate semi-arid conditions with irrigation
- **Altitude:** Up to 1500 m above sea level
- **Soil type:** Well-drained sandy loam to loam; pH 6.0–7.5
- **Humidity:** Moderate; high humidity during flowering reduces fruit set

## Recommended Guava Varieties (India)
- **L-49 (Sardar/Lucknow-49):** Most popular commercial variety; pink-fleshed; moderate disease tolerance
- **Allahabad Safeda:** White-fleshed; excellent flavor; moderately susceptible to diseases
- **IARI Selection-6 (G-6):** Good fruit quality; tolerant to wilt
- **Apple Colour Guava:** Red-skinned; high consumer value; susceptible to rust
- **CO-3 (Tamil Nadu):** Suitable for arid regions; compact growth
